20 Sketch Models
BACKGROUND

I am creating an interactive and modular light system. So in this 20-model iteration process, I am testing out what will be the best shape for creating 3D forms, and what will be the best joint type for assembling.

SHAPE TESTING

The first aspect I tested with was the right shape.
I tested 10 different shapes, and narrowed down to cylinderical and rectangular shapes to move forward.

Rectangular Sheets

8-Side Thin Slab

This shape is modified from the rectangular sheets, and is similar to the shape of leaves and petals. So several pieces connected together can create an end form that looks like flowers and trees.

Cubes
Triangular Prisms
Pyramids

Triangular Pyramids



Triangular Pyramid is a very interesting shape! It can be pieced together to create a clustered geometric shape; however, once you turn them at the joints they can transform into a linear form.

Cylinders

During testing with cylinders, the cylinders reminded me about the Russian Nesting Dolls.
My light system can also use this form of being portable while is packed,
but separately each individual piece can also be a building block on its own.
So I started dividing a cylinder, and make a big cylinder into many small cylinders.
