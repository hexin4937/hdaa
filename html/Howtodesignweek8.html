Lightgo
CONCEPT

Modular light building blocks

At beginning of this project, I got interested in interactive lighting. I have looked into many public light shows and home smart lighting systems.

Therefore, I decided to create modular lights that can be portable when interconnected, but also has the potential to be anything when expanded.

CHARACTERSTICS: Interactive, small, portable, easy connection.



CONTEXT

Playable Educational Lighting Blocks for Children

It is small and versatile. With it, children can learn about lighting and circuit. They will be able to figure out how to wire a complete circuit through playing with the blocks, as well as DIY their own desk lamp.

PHYSICAL MODELS

Shape = Cellular Packing

In order to discover the best shape for portable and expandable, I have tested different nesting forms.

Then I researched on cellular packing, and foudn out that most cellular packing uses triangles, cubes or hexagons as basic building blocks. Hexagon is also consisted of 6 equilateral triangles. Therefore, a nesting can be built energy efficiently from equilateral triangles.



Electronics

1. In Parallel


When the modules are in parallel, they light up one by one.


However, parallel modules have limitation in the freedom of connnection.

Also parallel modules will result in 2 different kinds of modules.

2. In Series


When the modules are in series, they light up when the circuit is completed.

In this video, it shows that connecting in series currently have a problem of not enough voltage when divided between different LEDs.

In series modules will have more degree of freedome. The module can rotate around the point of contact.


Thus, I still want to dig deeper in figuring out how to light up modules in series.
Some solutions I have in mind right now are to use variable voltage source or variable resistor to create a constant current in the circuit. These solution can also teach children the relationship between voltage, current and resistance in circuit.
